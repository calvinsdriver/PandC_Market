Regulatory moves, including NAIC's nationwide data call on homeowners insurance, signal heightened scrutiny on market accessibility, especially in high-risk states like California and Georgia, where surplus lines are gaining traction due to wildfire and climate concerns.

For the industry, these developments imply a cautious optimism: soft pricing could attract more policyholders but expose carriers to underpricing risks in a volatile environment, while tech innovations like AI-driven claims processes from firms like Verisk offer efficiency gains.
