Duck Creek Technologies has surged ahead in the P&C core systems market by releasing an insurance-native Agentic AI platform that embeds autonomous underwriting and claims tools alongside a Product Configurator promising 50% faster policy rollout. These launches, paired with new reinsurance management capabilities and integrations such as Zelis claims payments, underscore the vendor’s push to embed intelligence directly into legacy modernization projects. 

Multiple carriers including Encova, Country-Wide, Fortegra, and HDFC ERGO have selected Duck Creek modules in recent quarters, while the company earned Leader status in both the Gartner Magic Quadrant for SaaS P&C Core Platforms and the Everest Underwriting Orchestration matrix. Leadership refresh—new CEO Hardeep Gulati, CTO Rajesh Raheja, and CPO Jose Lazares—has coincided with these product and customer wins, signaling a more aggressive go-to-market stance against entrenched rivals.

Guidewire Software, by contrast, has drawn attention mainly for share-price volatility, repeated insider sales by CEO Michael Rosenbaum, and mixed analyst sentiment even as recurring-revenue growth and AI-SaaS narratives attract investor interest. Broader industry commentary from McKinsey and BCG highlights that agentic AI could unlock up to 90% productivity gains in core transformations, yet adoption remains uneven because many insurers still wrestle with budget discipline and integration complexity.

For carriers, the competitive edge is shifting toward platforms that combine configurable products, embedded AI agents, and proven implementation speed rather than traditional feature checklists. Investors should watch whether Duck Creek’s execution momentum translates into sustained market-share gains or whether Guidewire can convert its installed base into comparable AI-driven upsell velocity.
