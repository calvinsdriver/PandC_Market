Duck Creek Technologies has intensified its challenge to Guidewire by rolling out an insurance-native agentic AI platform that embeds autonomous tools for underwriting, claims, and product configuration, promising up to 50 percent faster policy implementation while adding dedicated reinsurance and multi-country capabilities. 

Recent customer selections by MAS in New Zealand, Frankmuth, HDFC ERGO, Fortegra, and Southern Trust underscore Duck Creek’s momentum in both SaaS migrations and specialized lines, coinciding with the appointment of former PowerSchool CEO Hardeep Gulati to steer an AI-first strategy. 

Guidewire, meanwhile, has seen its shares react to index inclusions and analyst commentary framing its cloud and AI investments as structural tailwinds rather than competitive threats, even as broader market commentary highlights the intensifying platform rivalry. 

McKinsey and Deloitte analyses point to agentic AI as the catalyst finally capable of modernizing entrenched core systems, a shift that favors vendors delivering measurable workflow automation over traditional replacement cycles. 

For investors and carriers, the pattern signals accelerating differentiation among core vendors, with early AI adopters positioned to capture modernization budgets while slower-moving platforms risk further commoditization.
