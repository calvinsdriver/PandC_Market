US property and casualty carriers delivered their strongest underwriting performance in years during the first quarter of 2026, generating a $16.3 billion underwriting gain as the combined ratio fell below 100 and net premiums written rose 2.8 percent year-over-year. Verisk and APCIA data show the sector sustaining this momentum into 2025, with Farmers posting 15.9 percent NPW growth and carriers such as Allstate, W.R. Berkley, and Markel Group reporting disciplined results that masked lingering property pressures.  

Rate cuts expected as early as September are easing investment-income headwinds, while PNC Bank’s new treasury-management solution for P&C payments and Zurich’s European life-sciences product launch illustrate how carriers are embedding technology and specialty lines to capture incremental revenue. Acquisitions by Amwins and Hilb Group, together with Great American’s new difference-in-conditions offering for California wineries, signal continued consolidation and product innovation aimed at underserved segments.  

Tariffs are inflating contents claims and climate-driven losses are rendering more assets uninsurable, widening the personalization gap between carriers and customers. These dynamics, coupled with the sector’s June equity rally—the strongest since 2022—suggest investors are rewarding underwriting discipline but will scrutinize exposure management and pricing agility as macro and weather risks intensify.
